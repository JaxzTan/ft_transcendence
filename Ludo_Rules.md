In Ludo, it takes 57 exact moves for each piece to travel from the starting position to the final home triangle.This distance is divided into two parts:52 spaces to complete one full lap around the main board.5 spaces to travel up your colored home column to the center.Since each player has 4 pieces, your goal is to make all 4 pieces complete this journey, requiring a total of 228 moves (\(57 \times 4\)) to win the game.Keep in mind that these numbers represent the absolute minimum theoretical moves. In an actual match, it usually takes significantly longer because pieces often get captured by opponents and have to restart their journey from the beginning.

 Full Ruleset for Classic Ludo
 
 Objective: Be the first player to move all four of your pieces from the Starting Area to the Home Triangle.
 
 Setup: Each player chooses one of the four colors (Red, Green, Yellow, Blue) and places their four pieces in their matching Starting Area.
 
 Turn Order: Play proceeds clockwise. Players take turns rolling a single six-sided die.Rolling and Entering the BoardEntering play: Pieces start locked in the Starting Area ("Prison"). To move a piece onto the starting track space, you must roll a 6.The "6" rule: Rolling a 6 grants you a bonus roll. You can use the 6 to enter a new piece or move a piece already on the track. If you roll three 6s in a row, your turn is immediately forfeited.
 
 Movement and Capturing
 
 Moving: You must move a piece forward clockwise the exact number of spaces shown on the die.
 
 Capturing: If your piece lands on a space occupied by an opponent's piece, the opponent's piece is captured. It is sent back to their Starting Area, and you receive a bonus roll.Blockades: If two or more of your own pieces land on the exact same space, they form a blockade. Opponents cannot pass or capture this blockade.
 
 🗺️ Breakdown of Board Positions
 
 A piece will encounter five distinct zones during its journey around the board.
 
 1. Starting Area ("Prison")What it is: The large, colored square courtyard in the corner of the board.Rules: Pieces here are inactive. They cannot move, cannot be captured, and cannot block anyone. They require a roll of 6 to leave.
 
 2. White Track (Main Pathway)What it is: The outer track consisting of 52 neutral white spaces shared by all players.Rules: This is the main battlefield. Normal movement, standard captures, and blockades apply here. Pieces travel clockwise around this track.
 
 3. Star Spaces (Safe Zones)What it is: Eight specific spaces on the main track marked with a star symbol (including each player's starting space).Rules: Pieces on these spaces are safe. They cannot be captured by opponents. If an opponent lands on your piece here, they simply share the space with you. Blockades cannot be formed on safe zones to stop others from landing there.
 
 4. Colored Columns (Home Stretch)What it is: A vertical row of 5 colored spaces matching your piece's color, leading directly to the center.Rules: Only pieces of the matching color can enter this column. Opponents cannot enter your colored column, meaning your pieces are 100% safe from capture here. Blockades are allowed here to slow down your own trailing pieces if needed.
 
 5. Home Triangle (The End)What it is: The large colored triangle in the exact center of the board.Rules: To enter, you must roll the exact number required to land on it. For example, if your piece is 3 spaces away, you must roll exactly a 3. If you roll a 4 or 5, your piece cannot move and your turn passes. Once a piece enters the triangle, it is permanently retired from play.
